Kim Dong Hyuk is an interdisciplinary filmmaker, researcher, and designer working at the intersection of computational technology and applied design. This dual focus is central to his current practice: at Hanyang University's Design Informatics Lab, he investigates the application of AI and HCI to design workflows, while as a project lead at FORUM D&P Inc., he merges cinematic techniques with architectural documentation.

His interest in computational creativity is reflected in his projects. He has developed a multimodal AI pipeline that applies computational methodologies to Cinematics, enabling a quantitative analysis of how specific shot compositions create emotional shifts. His work has also involved investigating the impact of 3D pre-visualization on the storyboarding process and utilizing generative ai tools to visualize narrative scenarios.

He applies this hybrid expertise to a dynamic creative practice. His work in filmmaking has been invited to and awarded at numerous international film festivals. In parallel, he contributes his visual and directorial skills to campaign projects for clients such as Adidas and the Korea National Contemporary Dance Company. Grounded in his dual studies in Theater & Film and Interior Architectural Design, Dong Hyuk consistently seeks to build unique worlds and craft compelling experiences.
